Water reaches its boiling point at 100°C (212°F) under standard atmospheric pressure, which occurs at sea level. This temperature is when the vapor pressure of water equals the surrounding air pressure, allowing bubbles to form and escape. At higher altitudes, where air pressure is lower, water boils at a lower temperature. This principle is essential in chemistry, cooking, and understanding weather patterns.



Roughly 60% of the human body is made up of water, playing a critical role in virtually every physiological process. Water regulates body temperature, helps transport nutrients and oxygen to cells, flushes out toxins, and lubricates joints. Brain tissue, blood, and muscles all rely heavily on hydration to function properly. Even mild dehydration can impair focus, mood, and physical performance.



Mount Everest, located in the Himalayas on the border of Nepal and China, stands as the tallest mountain above sea level, reaching an elevation of 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet). It was first summited in 1953 by Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay. Despite modern equipment, climbing Everest remains a deadly challenge due to altitude sickness, avalanches, and extreme weather.



Contrary to popular belief, the Great Wall of China is not visible from space without aid. Astronauts have confirmed that the wall blends into the natural terrain and is difficult to distinguish, especially with the naked eye. While some human-made structures like city lights or large airports can be seen from low Earth orbit, the wall’s color and width make it nearly invisible.



Bees play a fundamental role in global agriculture by pollinating more than 70% of the crops humans rely on for food, including fruits, vegetables, nuts, and seeds. Without bees, food production would drop significantly, threatening food security and biodiversity. Pollination also affects livestock, as many animal feed crops depend on bee activity. Protecting bee populations is critical for sustaining life on Earth.
